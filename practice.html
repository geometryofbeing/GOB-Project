The WholeShape

What shape are you in?

Each shape, when it takes over completely, shows you what the other two are for.

Circle — flow · connection · allowing

Too much circle

Absorbing everyone else's feelings. Open to everything, anchored to nothing. Arrived home empty.

Triangle — direction · purpose · action

Too much triangle

Moving fast, cutting through. So fixed on the destination that everything along the way is disappearing.

Square — ground · structure · integrity

Too much square

So certain you were right that the conversation ended before it began. Steady, but sealed.

The WholeShape

Integrated and purposeful and open — all at once. Three shapes, held together, instead of one at a time.

In relationship

The shapes between people

A relationship has a shape of its own — not just yours, not just theirs. It’s what takes shape in the space between you. The same question applies: which shape is showing up here, and which could use more room?

Conflict

When a conversation becomes all triangle

Both people sharp, pointed, pushing. The direction is real — but without circle there is no listening, and without square there is no ground to stand on together.

Find what you both care about. Add some ground. Then the direction has somewhere to go.

Care & caregiving

When circle becomes self-erasure

Responsive to everything except their own needs. Poured out for everyone else. Without square there is no self to return to, and without triangle no sense of one's own direction.

Care without boundaries, and without direction, runs empty.

Team & culture

When a team becomes all square

Structure and process in place. But without circle people feel unseen, and without triangle no one knows where they are going. The organization is orderly — and not alive.

Structure without relationship, and without purpose, produces effort without a shared direction.

In systems

The same three shapes, everywhere

Are all three shapes present and clear? Where one is missing, you’ll see it in what’s going wrong.

In NatureCircle, triangle, square as living forces — already in balance

You don’t have to argue for the shapes in nature — they’re just there to notice. Circle, triangle, and square show up in natural systems not as categories, but as forces already at work, already belonging to each other.

A one-of-a-kind glass WholeShape exists as a contemplative offering, not a product. Held up to sunlight, the form catches light, casts shadow, and reveals its own geometry.

The shapes don’t need to be explained when light is doing the showing.

A river

Circle in the flow — finding its way around every obstacle. Triangle in the current — always moving in a direction. Square in the banks — the stable ground that holds it.

Remove the banks and it floods. Remove the current and it stagnates. Remove the flow and it cuts straight through without sensing what it meets. All three are necessary. All three are already there.

A tree

Circle in the canopy — spreading in all directions, receiving light from everywhere. Triangle in the form — reaching upward, the roots reaching down. Square in the trunk — the steady structure that holds everything together.

The tree doesn’t choose between spreading and growing and standing firm. It does all three at once. That is the WholeShape — not as a concept, but as a living form.

A seed

Square in the containment — everything held, protected, waiting. Circle in the opening — softening, receiving water, beginning to respond. Triangle in the shoot — directed upward, toward light, with purpose.

The whole arc of growth in three shapes. Not in sequence — simultaneously, each enabling the others. Circle, triangle, and square as one living process.

Emotional IntelligenceHow the shapes work in childhood, and in adult life

In practice, the shapes seem to land before words do — with kids and adults alike. A child responds to circle, triangle, and square before they can name them. Adults notice the same thing under stress: the felt sense of a shape shows up before the thought does.

In childhood, the shapes offer a language before language. In adult life, they offer a language beneath language — something to reach for when words fail or mislead.

A child dissolved in others’ feelings — too much circle

“Too much circle?”

Without square there is no ground to return to, and without triangle no sense of their own direction. Absorbed into everyone else.

“What do you need — and where are you headed?”

A child shut down and immovable — too much square

“Very square right now?”

Without circle nothing can reach them, and without triangle there is no movement. The square that should be a foundation has become a fortress.

“Can you soften — and let something move?”

A child sharp, reactive, pushing against everything — too much triangle

“All triangle?”

Without square there is nowhere to land, and without circle no one to land with. Real energy with nowhere purposeful to go.

“Direction needs ground beneath it — and connection to make it matter.”

In adult life

The adult who can’t stop giving — too much circle

“You pour yourself out for everyone. But there is no self left to return to.”

Without square there is no ground, and without triangle no sense of one’s own direction. The care is real — and unsustainable.

“What do you need — and where are you headed?”

The adult who can’t be reached — too much square

“Nothing gets in. Nothing moves. Safe — and alone.”

Without circle there is no connection, and without triangle no forward movement. The walls are real — and they keep out the things that would help.

“Can you soften — and let something move?”

The adult who can’t stop moving — too much triangle

“Always forward. Never still. Arrived somewhere — and don’t know why.”

Without circle there is no connection to what matters, and without square no stability beneath the movement. The energy is real — and it has no home.

“Direction needs ground beneath it — and connection to make it matter.”

How We Lead Each OtherWhat happens when one shape runs the room

Most people have been in a room where one shape was running everything. And most people have been the one running it. The shapes don’t require a title to apply.

When circle runs the room — all listening, no direction, no ground

“Everyone felt heard. And nothing moved.”

Circle without triangle offers no direction, and without square nothing holds. People feel cared for — and leave anyway.

When square runs the room — all structure, no warmth, no direction

“The structure was clear. Nobody felt seen.”

Square without circle leaves no one feeling seen, and without triangle there is nowhere to go. The organization is orderly — and not alive.

When triangle runs the room — all momentum, no ground, no connection

“The energy was real. And it went everywhere at once.”

Triangle without circle means no one is listened to, and without square the purpose has nothing to anchor to. The energy is real — and without the other two, it becomes energy without shape.

When all three are present.

People feel seen. The structure holds. There is somewhere worth going. Not because of who is in charge — because circle, triangle, and square are all in the room.

FamilyEvery family has a geometry — which shape dominates in yours?

Every family has a geometry. The shapes don’t cause the patterns — they make them visible. Which one tends to dominate in yours?

Too much circle — everyone's feelings, no one's limits

“Everyone's needs mattered. Except somehow nobody's did.”

Without square there are no boundaries, and without triangle there is no direction. The warmth is real — and exhausting.

Too much square — rules without warmth

“We knew exactly what was expected. We just never felt seen.”

Without circle no one feels known, and without triangle there is nothing to move toward together. The structure holds — and nothing grows.

Too much triangle — always moving, never arriving

“There was always a next thing. The present never felt like enough.”

Without circle no one feels connected, and without square nothing is stable enough to return to. The momentum is real — and nobody can land.

Other areas of applicationHealthcare, grief, organizations, justice, government, the environment

The same three questions apply in any domain: Where is the circle? Where is the square? Where is the triangle? Where one is missing, you’ll see it in what’s going wrong.

Healthcare

Patient-centred care (circle), clinical protocols (square), purpose of care (triangle).

Grief

Feeling it fully (circle), the structure and ritual that holds you through it (square), the slow return of direction (triangle).

Organizations

Culture and trust (circle), process and governance (square), strategy and purpose (triangle).

Restorative justice

Relationship repair (circle), accountability and consequence (square), forward movement (triangle).

Government

Community and relationship (circle), policy, infrastructure and regulation (square), direction, vision and values (triangle).

Environmental Stewardship

Relationship to the natural world (circle), sustainable practice and care (square), conservation and direction (triangle).
